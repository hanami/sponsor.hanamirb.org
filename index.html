Dear #rubyfriends—

For over a decade (a billion gem installs and counting!), Hanami, Dry, and Rom have been elevating your Ruby.

We’ve elevated your apps, giving you drop-in tools for strict typing, flexible validation, business logic handling, database layers and more, all the way to a batteries-included framework.

We’ve elevated your skills, helping you blend functional and object oriented styles and create clearer data flows and separations of concern, leading to maintainable architectures that are a pleasure to work on.

And we’ve elevated Ruby itself, providing essential variety through our technical innovation and friendly, inclusive community.

Now we’re preparing to take the Hanami family to the next level. This year, we will:

  • Merge Hanami with Dry and Rom to create a single compelling Ruby toolkit backed by a unified community and maintainer team.
  • Focus on helping our users become more successful with our tools.
  • Establish paid ongoing maintenance of Hanami for the very first time.

Together, these will put Hanami on a sustainable trajectory for our second decade and beyond. We’re here for the long run.

We need your help to make this happen! We need you and your Ruby business to become Hanami patrons.

We’d love for you to join Mike Perham, Honeybadger, Brandon Weaver and FastRuby in ensuring a sustainable future for Hanami, and a more diverse Ruby ecosystem.

— Tim Riley
Hanami project lead
